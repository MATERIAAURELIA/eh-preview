So… How Was Your First Sound Bath?

Marcus Fung · Apr 18, 2026 · 2 min read

Hi friends, this is a short point-form article that I wanted to roll out right after tonight's community sound bath. I'm going to rebuild it when I have more dedicated time.

Experience vs Practice

  • Is the sound bath an experience, a practice, or a class?
  • When we frame it as an experience → it's either "good" or "bad"
  • When we frame it as a practice → we open up to what it can teach us about ourselves
  • A practice gives us a pathway of rich insight, discovery, and a place to apply tools to work through what comes up

When It Doesn't Go "Well"

  • Sometimes the sound is enough to carry us through — and sometimes it isn't
  • Every experience is different, so don't be discouraged
  • A challenging session isn't a failure — it's an invitation to learn more about the information and feedback your body is giving you
  • Try again. You'll get better at it. There are things that can help.

This is where I'm headed with this... let me know if it's helpful.

The Bigger Question

  • These things likely don't just come up in the sound bath
  • They come up in life — the sound bath helped to make them visible
  • You came to relax — and sometimes the first thing that surfaces is everything that's been blocking the relaxation
